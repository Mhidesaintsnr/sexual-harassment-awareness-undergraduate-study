# Sexual Harassment Reporting Awareness Among LASU Undergraduates

A quantitative survey study examining the extent to which social media awareness campaigns influence Lagos State University (LASU) undergraduates' knowledge of — and willingness to use — official sexual harassment reporting channels.

---

## Overview

Sexual harassment remains an underreported problem in Nigerian university environments. This study investigates whether university-led social media awareness content meaningfully improves students' knowledge of reporting channels and their willingness to use them, using a structured survey administered across all five study levels at LASU.

---

## Research Questions

1. To what extent are LASU undergraduates aware of official channels for reporting sexual harassment?
2. Which social media platforms are being used by the university to disseminate awareness of these channels?
3. Does awareness created through social media influence students' willingness to use official reporting channels?

## 2. Social Media Platform Usage
- **69 respondents (34.3%)** had encountered university sexual harassment awareness content on social media
- WhatsApp was the dominant platform, cited by 42 of those who had seen awareness content
- WhatsApp also leads as the most-used platform generally (129/201 respondents, 64.2%)

### 3. Influence of Awareness on Willingness to Report

| Group | Aware of a Reporting Channel | Willing to Use One |
|---|---|---|
| Exposed to awareness content (n=69) | 55 (79.7%) | 48 (69.6%) |
| Not exposed (n=130) | 40 (30.8%) | 28 (21.5%) |

- Exposed students were **nearly 3x more likely** to know a reporting channel (79.7% vs 30.8%)
- Willingness to report was also significantly higher among exposed students (69.6% vs 21.5%)

## Implications

- Any university awareness strategy that does not prioritise **WhatsApp** is missing the channel students already default to
- Social media exposure has a stronger effect on **channel awareness** than on willingness to report — suggesting that other factors (institutional trust, confidentiality concerns, past experience) also shape reporting behaviour
- The awareness gap is widest among junior students (100–200 Level), pointing to an early-intervention opportunity

---
## Recommendations
- Increase awareness campaigns across social media.
- Use WhatsApp more frequently.
- Conduct compulsory awareness programs for first-year students.
- Improve visibility of reporting procedures.
